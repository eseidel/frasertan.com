## Ingredients

1 1/3 cups (213g) all purpose flour
1 1/2 tsp baking powder
12 tbsp (1 1/2 sticks) unsalted butter
1 1/2 cups (300g) brown sugar
3/4 tsp table salt
1 1/2 tbsp pure vanilla extract
1 large egg, room temperature
3/4 cup (90g) toasted pecans, broken into bite sized pieces
3/4 cup (128g) semisweet chocolate chips
1/2 cup (85g) white chocolate chips
Equipment
9" x 13" baking pan

## Instructions

Set oven rack to middle position and preheat oven to 350°. Grease a 9" x 13" baking pan, line with parchment to form a sling, and grease parchment.

In a medium bowl, whisk together flour and baking powder.

In a medium-sized saucepan, melt the butter sugar and salt together over medium heat: stir until smooth. Off heat, stir in the vanilla. Let mixture cool to room temperature.

Add the egg and whisk until combined. Add the flour mixture and stir until just combined. Add in the pecans and chocolate chips and stir gently.

Press the batter into the pan, patting into a thin even layer across the bottom of the pan.

Bake for 18 to 24 minutes, until the blondies are set on the edge, the top is golden brown and the top is just beginning to crack. A toothpick inserted into the center should come out with a few crumbs.

Transfer pan to a wire rack and let cool completely in the pan. When cool, use the sling to transfer to a cutting board. Cut into bars. Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days.

## Notes

- If you want more chocolate kick, replace the white chocolate chips with equal amounts chocolate chips.
- I prefer to break up my toasted pecans by hand - I find this results in less nut dust, and more evenly sized bits.
- If you use the right sized saucepan you can do the mixing therein - my 3 qt Great Jones Saucy saucepan worked well.
