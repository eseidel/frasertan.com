## Ingredients

2 1/2 cups (12 1/2 ounces) all-purpose flour
5 teaspoons baking powder
1/2 teaspoon salt
1 heaping cup mashed bananas (3 large ones)
3 cups milk
2 cups (6 ounces) old-fashioned rolled oats
4 large eggs
6 tablespoons vegetable oil
6 tablespoons sugar
Equipment
griddle

## Instructions

In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder and salt.

Combine the mashed bananas, oats and milk in a medium bowl. Let sit until the oats are softened, about 5 minutes.

Add the eggs, oil and sugar to bowl with banana mixture and whisk until well combined.

Add banana mixture to flour mixture. Whisk just until combined.

Heat griddle to medium heat. Use a 1/4 or 1/3 cup measure to scoop portions on to the griddle to cook. (Optionally, sprinkle mini chocolate chips onto the pancakes after pouring, before the first flip.)

Note that these are pretty thick and hefty pancakes, so they'll need longer time on lower heat to cook all the way through without burning. I found turning both burners to just below medium and cooking for 3-3:30 per side worked well.

## Notes

- I doubled the recipe to use my "standard" number of mashed bananas.
- That's a lot of milk! Instead, I use 3 cups of water + 9 tablespoons (or a heaping half cup) of Bob's Red Mill Non-fat Milk Powder.
